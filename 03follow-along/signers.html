Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence. Later became the third President of the United States (1801–1809).
John Adams
Key advocate for independence during the Continental Congress. Served as the second President of the United States (1797–1801).
Benjamin Franklin
Renowned polymath, inventor, and diplomat. Played a critical role in securing French support during the Revolutionary War.
John Hancock
President of the Second Continental Congress at the time of the signing. Known for his large and prominent signature.
Samuel Adams
A key organizer of the Boston Tea Party and a leader in the push for independence. Often called the "Father of the American Revolution."
Richard Henry Lee
Introduced the motion for independence in the Continental Congress. Served as a leading Virginia statesman.
Roger Sherman
Only person to sign all four major founding documents of the United States: the Declaration of Independence, Articles of Association, Articles of Confederation, and the Constitution.
James Wilson
Played a significant role in drafting the Declaration. Later became a key figure in framing the U.S. Constitution.

These men, along with others, were instrumental in laying the foundation for the United States as an independent nation.
